LLM Fundamentals cover two main areas: understanding how LLMs work and knowing their capabilities and limitations. Here's a breakdown:

1. How LLMs Work:

  • Neural Networks: LLMs rely on a specific type of neural network architecture called transformers [Ask HN: How to Learn LLM Fundamentals].
    These complex mathematical models learn patterns from massive amounts of text data.
  • Training: The LLM is exposed to vast amounts of text and code during training. It analyzes the relationships between words, sentences, and paragraphs to understand
    how language works.
  • Parameters: The complexity of an LLM is measured in parameters similar to settings in a complex machine. More parameters allow for more nuanced understanding
    and text generation.

2. LLM Capabilities and Limitations:

  • Text Generation: LLMs excel at generating human-quality text, including different writing styles, creative text formats like poems or code, and even translating languages.
  • Emergent Abilities: As a byproduct of their training, LLMs can perform tasks they weren't explicitly trained for, like question answering or text summarization.
  • Challenges: LLMs can be expensive to train and require significant computational resources. They may also output factually incorrect information (hallucination)
    or biased content based on their training data.
